With the use of minesweeper functions to explore (tap), mark (flag), and query the constraints (status and hints), we can now put them together in a program in a Swift playground to solve the minesweeper. Constraints are sometimes not available and we will need to guess and explore if guessing in the center is better than guessing on the sides. You are welcome to edit and write your own guessing routine and/or change the minesweeper density to check the success of the program.
